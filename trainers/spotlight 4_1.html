My name is Danielle Pearson. I live in Wisconsin, USA. My school is called Danver Elementary School and I really like it there. School starts at 8:30. 🐋 A Whale of a Time! — Grey Whales

Grey whales, often called 'friendly whales', make the same amazing journey every year!

🌊 April to November: The whales stay in the cold waters of the Arctic looking for food.

🌴 December to April: They go to the warmer waters of Mexico to have their babies!

The Grey whale travels more miles than any other mammal on earth. They don't need tickets, passports or suitcases — and I'm sure they all have a whale of a time!

🥨 What Would You Like for Your Tea?

Sushki, baranki, bubliki (bagels) are traditional Russian products. You need flour, butter, oil, water, salt and sugar to make them. They can be round or oval, very big or very small and they can have different colours and flavours.

They are very popular because they taste good and they are cheap. Sushki last a long time too. People like to take sushki with them when they go hiking.

Everybody loves sushki, baranki and bubliki: from babies to old people. They are a good treat at teatime.

🦬 Animals Need Our Help — WWF

WWF — World Wildlife Fund — helps to save the wildlife on our planet. In Russia there are 100 reserves and 33 national parks where different kinds of animals live.

Near Ryazan and Moscow there are bison reserves where WWF supporters have a special project, called "Adopt a Bison". People donate money to buy food, pay vets and raise a baby bison. They can give a name to it, too. Every adopter gets a Certificate of Adoption. July 2 is "A Day of Adopters" in the bison reserves.

📖 Grammar Reference

1. Present Continuous — Настоящее продолженное

Formation: am/is/are + verb-ing

Use: Actions happening now, at the moment

Signal words: now, at the moment, Look!

PositiveNegativeQuestion
I am playing
I'm playing		I am not playing
I'm not playing		Am I playing?
He is playing
He's playing		He is not playing
He isn't playing		Is he playing?
Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.
They are playing
They're playing		They are not playing
They aren't playing		Are they playing?
Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.

2. Present Simple — Настоящее простое

Formation: verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)

Use: Regular actions, habits, facts

Signal words: every day, always, usually, often, sometimes, never, on Mondays

Spelling rules (3rd person singular):

RuleExample
Most verbs: add -seat → eats, play → plays
Verbs in -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -esmiss → misses, finish → finishes, watch → watches, go → goes
Consonant + y → -iesstudy → studies, cry → cries
Vowel + y: add -splay → plays

Pronunciation of -s/-es:

/s/ — after voiceless sounds: he eats
/iz/ — after sibilants: he watches
/z/ — after vowels and voiced sounds: he goes

3. Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Present Simple: She works from 9 to 5 every day. (regular)

Present Continuous: George isn't sleeping at the moment. He is at the cinema. He is watching a film with his friends. (now)

4. Prepositions of Place

5. Have got / Has got

Use: Possession, appearance

I/You/We/They have got (I've got)

He/She/It has got (He's got)

He's got fair hair. She's got blue eyes.

6. Can / Can't — Ability

I can swim really well.
He can't dive.
Can you ride a bike? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.

7. Have to / Don't have to

Have to: Necessity (you must do it)
Don't have to: No necessity (not required)

I have to be at school at 9 o'clock.
Peter doesn't have to go to school on Saturday.
Does she have to work at night? — Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.

8. Much / Many / A lot of

Much: Uncountable nouns (in questions & negatives) — milk, cheese, sugar
Many: Countable nouns plural (in questions & negatives) — books, apples
A lot of: Both (in positive sentences)
How much / How many — for questions

9. May — Permission

May I...? — formal request for permission
Answer: Certainly. / Of course you may. / Yes, you may. / No, you may not.

10. Comparatives

Short adjectives: add -er (tall → taller, big → bigger, funny → funnier)
Long adjectives: use more (popular → more popular)
Irregular: good → better, bad → worse
Use with: than

11. Must / Mustn't

Must: Obligation (you have to do it)
Mustn't: Prohibition (you can't do it)

I must put my rubbish in the bin.
You mustn't run. / You mustn't feed the animals.
